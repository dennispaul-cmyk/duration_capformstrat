Section I — Frame

How I'm thinking about this

After meeting with Josh in Montecito and multiple meetings and discussions with Emmett over the past 6 months or more — plus a bit of help on his recent trip to Australia — the question that excites me most is: how can I help you both shape and expand Duration's next chapter? You've built a remarkable franchise; the question now is how the three of us take it forward together. Capital Formation is the most immediate piece of that work, and the note below is how I'd propose the three of us think about organizing it — the vehicles, the LP universes, and the cadence — with Nate and the current internal IR team in support, and in conjunction perhaps with external resources where specific markets or use cases call for them.

Three vehicles to think about in parallel — TIF II, TCIP (or its successor structure), and the co-invest channel — and three distinct LP universes that map to each. But it is important to note, especially with FOs, that you can't predict exactly which of these three structures (or combination of one or more) might appeal to each family — so the conversation needs to stay flexible and present the full menu rather than pre-sort each FO into a single bucket.

A few framing points before the lists.

TIF I has 32 LPs. That's a small base relative to a $4.9bn franchise. The institutional headroom for TIF II is real, and a lot of it sits in places PH covers well — but a lot of it also sits in places PH doesn't naturally reach: family offices with institutional-scale checks, sovereign-adjacent FOs, and the Asia and Middle East FO universe where I have direct relationships.

TCIP is the FO vehicle. The deck literally says "invest alongside family and pension fund owners." 10+ year holds, 3–5% cash yield, perpetual capital structure — that's a family office product. The institutional consultants will gravitate to TIF II's closed-end format. The FO channel is where TCIP grows, and that's where I lead.

Co-invest is the gateway. Watco, Ports America, Mainstay — every FO conversation I'd have starts with "would you like to look at a single deal first" before fund commitments. The co-invest channel feeds TCIP, and TCIP commitments lead to TIF II conversations.

"In terms of Capital Formation, a key aspect of my Operating Partner role will be as a strategic thought partner with both of you on LPs and how to best approach them, to open the doors my network can open, and then to work closely with Nate and the internal IR team to manage the pipeline end-to-end — bringing each of you in where your presence can make the most difference."

A note on working together

This is a draft and most of it will change after we talk. The point is to get a structure on paper so the three of us are working from the same map — honest about where my network adds the most, where the existing IR team is the better-placed channel, and where PH might or might not earn its fee, or where other external resources might be needed to augment what we can do internally. The bigger picture is what Duration becomes over the next decade, and how I can come in and play an additive role to both of you in the continued evolution of the firm you've created.

Section II — Park Hill

The Park Hill question

As I understand it, the EL with PH may not yet be signed — though it's possible the decision has already been made on your end without my involvement, which is entirely fine. Either way, in my opinion this is a decision that should be settled within the next month or so, because the answer shapes everything downstream — my role, IR resourcing, the consultant outreach plan, and where the dollars get spent over the next 18 months. The notes below are my honest read of the trade-off, offered as input rather than as advocacy in either direction.

What PH does well

PH has direct coverage relationships with the major US/Canadian pensions, Korean and Japanese institutionals, and the European LPs that allocate via consultants. Their team builds the meeting calendar, manages diligence requests, and runs the standard process work that absorbs IR bandwidth. For a first independent fund post-spin from Oaktree, PH provides institutional cover and a clear playbook.

Where the question opens up

PH books meetings; PH does not write checks. Decision-makers sit at the LPs. On a $1.5bn raise, PH economics are roughly $30m. Duration is $4.9bn AUM with an existing IR team (Nate Quinn full-time on IR, Will Frew supporting, plus the broader senior team). The question is whether $30m buys enough incremental AUM commitments that would not otherwise close on our own and that amount to more than $30m in fees down the road, or whether a portion of that economic value can be captured internally with senior-led process management.

No view from me on the answer — but the implications for how I'd organize my work look different in each case:

A
If PH is engaged
My role is complementary and additive — focused on the FO universe (US, ME, Asia, EU) and the consultant relationships PH does not own. The three of us would shape how the TCIP/co-invest channel runs as primary, and I'd take the lead on supplying incremental TIF II capital from sources PH would not naturally reach. Clear LP-by-LP carve-up between PH and us to avoid overlap or LP confusion.
B
If PH is not engaged
My role is broader. The three of us would together shape and expand Duration's institutional outreach process — building on what you've already established, scaling the existing IR team's coverage, managing the consultant-led institutional channels (StepStone, Albourne, Hamilton Lane, Mercer), and adding the FO and HNW channels where my network is the natural fit. The IR team likely needs one or two additions over time, but the economics of internal-led vs. PH-led look very different on a 12–18 month process — and the institutional muscle Duration builds in the process is a permanent asset for every fund that follows.

Worth treating as a real fork in the road, not a default. Happy to go deeper in person. The lists below work either way.
